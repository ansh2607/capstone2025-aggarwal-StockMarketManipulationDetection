<P STYLE="text-align: right; margin: 0"><B>Exhibit 99.1</B></P>

<P STYLE="margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><B>Plug Power&nbsp;to Restate&nbsp;Previously
Issued Financial Statements</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><I>No Expected&nbsp;Impact on Cash&nbsp;Position,
Business Operations or&nbsp;Economics&nbsp;of&nbsp;Commercial&nbsp;Arrangements</I>&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><I>Plug Power&nbsp;Remains Well Positioned
to Capitalize on its Leadership&nbsp;in the Rapidly Growing&nbsp;Global Green&nbsp;Hydrogen&nbsp;and&nbsp;Fuel Cell&nbsp;Industry</I>&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0"><B>LATHAM, N.Y., March 16, 2021</B>&nbsp;-- Plug Power Inc.
(NASDAQ: PLUG), a leading provider of&nbsp;turnkey&nbsp;hydrogen solutions building the global green hydrogen economy, announced
today that it will restate its previously issued financial statements for fiscal years 2018 and 2019 and its quarterly filings
for 2019 and 2020, which will be disclosed in the Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2020 (the &ldquo;Prior Period Financial
Statements&rdquo;).&nbsp;&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In&nbsp;consultation with&nbsp;KPMG&nbsp;LLP, the Company's
independent registered public accounting firm,&nbsp;management and the Audit Committee of&nbsp;Plug Power&rsquo;s Board&nbsp;of
Directors&nbsp;determined that the Company&rsquo;s&nbsp;Prior Period Financial Statements need to be restated due to&nbsp;errors
in accounting primarily&nbsp;related to several non-cash items, including:&nbsp;&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 0.75in; text-indent: 0in"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Symbol">&middot;</FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family: Times New Roman, Times, Serif">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The reported book value of right of use assets and related finance obligations;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 0.75in; text-indent: 0in"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Symbol">&middot;</FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family: Times New Roman, Times, Serif">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Loss accruals for certain service contracts;&nbsp;&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 0.75in; text-indent: 0in"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Symbol">&middot;</FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family: Times New Roman, Times, Serif">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The&nbsp;impairment&nbsp;of&nbsp;certain long-lived&nbsp;assets; and&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 0.75in; text-indent: 0in"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Symbol">&middot;</FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family: Times New Roman, Times, Serif">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The classification of certain costs, resulting in a decrease in&nbsp;research and development expense and a corresponding increase
in&nbsp;cost of revenue.&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">The accounting related to the restatement is complex and technical
and involves significant judgments in how to apply U.S. GAAP, given the innovative nature of the Company&rsquo;s business and its&nbsp;leading&nbsp;position
in a new and rapidly developing industry.&nbsp;The revised accounting will change how the Company accounts for certain transactions
and items, but&nbsp;is not expected to&nbsp;impact&nbsp;the Company&rsquo;s cash position, business operations or economics of
commercial&nbsp;arrangements.<FONT STYLE="color: red">&nbsp;</FONT>The Company continues to expect to achieve its previously stated
gross billings targets of $475 million in 2021, $750 million in 2022 and $1.7 billion in 2024.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Andy Marsh, CEO of Plug Power, said,&nbsp;&ldquo;Since our founding
nearly 25 years ago, Plug Power has prided itself on operating with transparency and integrity, and we are&nbsp;working&nbsp;to&nbsp;resolve&nbsp;this
matter quickly.&nbsp;Importantly,&nbsp;there is no expected impact to&nbsp;our&nbsp;cash position, business operations or economics
of commercial arrangements.&nbsp;We continue to execute on our mission&nbsp;to&nbsp;provide customers with state-of-the-art fuel
cell and&nbsp;green&nbsp;hydrogen solutions. We remain&nbsp;confident in our ability to&nbsp;leverage&nbsp;our&nbsp;strong business
momentum and market leading technologies, independently and alongside our joint venture partners, to capture the significant business
opportunities in this rapidly growing&nbsp;industry.&rdquo;&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">As part of the Company&rsquo;s normal process, prior to releasing&nbsp;its&nbsp;2020
fourth quarter and year end&nbsp;preliminary&nbsp;results and prior to completion of the audit,&nbsp;on February 24, 2021,&nbsp;the
Company and the Audit Committee discussed those results with&nbsp;KPMG, and at that time, no material issues were raised. However,
after&nbsp;the Company&nbsp;reported&nbsp;its 2020 fourth quarter and year end results,&nbsp;and in the course of finalizing the
audit, the Company and KPMG identified&nbsp;the restatement items cited above. The Company&nbsp;has since&nbsp;reevaluated its
accounting&nbsp;and determined that&nbsp;it needed to correct&nbsp;the previous accounting for those items.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">It is important to note that&nbsp;the&nbsp;changes being recorded&nbsp;did&nbsp;not
result from any override of controls or misconduct,&nbsp;and KPMG has not informed the&nbsp;Audit Committee of any issues related
to an override of controls or misconduct.&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">The Company&nbsp;will not be able to file its Form 10-K
for&nbsp;2020&nbsp;by the March 16, 2021 deadline&nbsp;and&nbsp;is working diligently&nbsp;to finalize the restated
financials&nbsp;and file&nbsp;its&nbsp;Form 10-K&nbsp;as&nbsp;soon as&nbsp;possible. As disclosed in the Company&rsquo;s
Current Report on Form 8-K filed today with the SEC, the Prior Period Financial Statements should no longer be relied upon
and the fourth quarter and full year 2020 financial results and related discussion in the Company&rsquo;s shareholder letter
issued on February 25, 2021 should no longer be relied upon.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0"><B>About Plug Power</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Plug Power is building the hydrogen economy as the leading provider
of comprehensive hydrogen fuel cell turnkey solutions. The Company&rsquo;s innovative technology powers electric motors with hydrogen
fuel cells amid an ongoing paradigm shift in the power, energy, and transportation industries to address climate change and energy
security, while meeting sustainability goals. Plug Power created the first commercially viable market for hydrogen fuel cell technology.
As a result, the Company has deployed over 40,000 fuel cell systems for e-mobility, more than anyone else in the world, and has
become the largest buyer of liquid hydrogen, having built and operated a hydrogen highway across North America. Plug Power delivers
a significant value proposition to end-customers, including meaningful environmental benefits, efficiency gains, fast fueling,
and lower operational costs. Plug Power&rsquo;s vertically-integrated GenKey solution ties together all critical elements to power,
fuel, and provide service to customers such as Amazon, BMW, The Southern Company, Carrefour, and Walmart. The Company is now leveraging
its know-how, modular product architecture and foundational customers to rapidly expand into other key markets including zero-emission
on-road vehicles, robotics, and data centers. Learn more at www.plugpower.com.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Gross billings is based on the invoice value of equipment deployed
in the period. To that amount, the Company adds the invoice value for services rendered in the period, fuel provided to customers,
power provided under Power Purchase agreements, etc. Invoice value of equipment and service is measured on a relative basis using
cash value within contracts with customers. The Company&rsquo;s objective in presenting gross billings is to present to investors
an operating metric that conveys commercial growth over time. Management also uses this operating metric as a measurement of commercial
growth, as well as establishing performance targets and annual budgets, and makes operating decisions based in part on gross billings.
The significant estimates and assumptions underlying the metric include the allocation of revenue, excluding the provision for
warrants, based on relative stand along selling prices used in our GAAP revenue numbers.</P>
