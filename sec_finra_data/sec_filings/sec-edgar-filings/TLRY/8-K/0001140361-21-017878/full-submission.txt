    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;">As previously disclosed in the Current Reports on Forms 8-K and 8-K/A filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission on May 4, 2021, by Tilray,
      Inc., a Delaware corporation (the &#8220;<span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">Company</span>&#8221;), the Company consummated a plan of arrangement under the Business Corporations Act (Ontario) (the &#8220;<span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">Arrangement</span>&#8221;) with Aphria Inc., a corporation existing under the laws of the Province of Ontario (&#8220;<span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">Aphria</span>&#8221;), on April 30, 2021.<a id="z_Hlk72010101"></a></div>

    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;">On May 13, 2021, the Company announced that, as part of its plan to combine operations with Aphria and enhance operational efficiencies following
      the consummation of the Arrangement, it will be closing its High Park Farms facility in Petrolia, Ontario. The closure is expected to be completed by September 2021. There are approximately 200 employees at High Park Farms in Petrolia, and the
      Company is encouraging employees to seek opportunities at its nearby cultivation and manufacturing operations in Leamington and London, Ontario, where the Company expects to concentrate local cultivation and local manufacturing, respectively.</div>
