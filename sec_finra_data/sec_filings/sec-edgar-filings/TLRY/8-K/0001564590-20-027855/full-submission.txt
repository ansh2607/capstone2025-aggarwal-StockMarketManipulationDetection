<p style="Background-color:#FFFFFF;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:6pt;text-indent:4.54%;color:#000000;font-size:10pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">On May 26, 2020, Tilray, Inc. (&#8220;Tilray&#8221;) announced that it will permanently shut down its facility at High Park Gardens, a licensed cannabis facility in Leamington, Ontario, operated through its<span style="color:#404040;"> wholly-owned subsidiary Natura Naturals Inc. </span>(&#8220;High Park Gardens&#8221;). This High Park Gardens closure is a result of changing industry dynamics and evolving business needs. High Park Gardens <span style="color:#3B3838;">will close its operations over the next six weeks and such closure is expected to be completed by end of July 2020. As a result of the closure, Tilray expects to realize annualized net savings of approximately $7.5 million (current production costs net of future third- party purchases and ongoing depreciation) and avoid significant ongoing capital expenditures. </span></p>
<p style="Background-color:#FFFFFF;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:12pt;text-indent:4.54%;color:#000000;font-size:10pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Tilray estimates that the closure of High Park Gardens will result in termination costs primarily related to severance of approximately&#160;$300,000&#160;to be recorded in 2020. In connection with the permanent shutdown of High Park Gardens, Tilray anticipates non-cash charges related to (i) its property, plant and equipment in relation to placing these assets for sale and valuing such at their fair value less normal selling costs, (ii) inventory in relation to destroying of unharvested flower, and (iii) writing down to nil its intangible assets primarily related to the acquired production license. Tilray expects to recognize&#160;non-cash&#160;charges ranging from approximately&#160;$18.0 million to $28.0&#160;million&#160;resulting from these write-downs and impairment charges in the second quarter ending June&#160;30, 2020.</p>
