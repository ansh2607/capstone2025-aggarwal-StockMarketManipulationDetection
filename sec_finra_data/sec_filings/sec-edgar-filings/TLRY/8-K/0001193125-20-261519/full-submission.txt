In addition, the provisions of the Certificate and Delaware law that entitled the holders of shares of Class&#160;2 and Class&#160;1 common stock, in certain circumstances, to separate class voting rights are no longer applicable as a result of the conversion.</p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:justify"><span style="font-style:italic">Economic Interests.</span><span style="font-style:italic"></span>&#160;The conversion had no impact on the economic interests of holders of shares of Class&#160;1 common stock, including with regard to dividends, liquidation rights, treatment in connection with a change of control or merger transaction and redemption.</p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:justify"><span style="font-style:italic">Capitalization.</span><span style="font-style:italic"></span>&#160;The conversion had no impact on the total number of the Company&#8217;s issued and outstanding shares of capital stock; the shares of Class&#160;1 common stock converted into an equivalent number of shares of Class&#160;2 common stock. However, the Company&#8217;s total number of authorized shares of capital stock was reduced from 760,000,000 to 743,333,333 to account for the elimination of the 16,666,667 shares of Class&#160;1 common stock that were issued prior to the conversion. The Company&#8217;s authorized capital stock consists of 500,000,000 shares of Class&#160;2 common stock, 233,333,333 shares of Class&#160;1 common stock and 10,000,000 shares of preferred stock, par value $0.0001 per share. The Company&#8217;s Certificate prohibits the issuance of any shares of Class&#160;1 common stock.</p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:justify"><span style="font-style:italic">Resale of Common Stock.</span><span style="font-style:italic"></span>&#160;Shares of Class&#160;2 common stock may be sold in the same manner as such shares and shares of Class&#160;1 common stock were previously sold prior to the conversion.
