<p style="text-align:right;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-weight:bold;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Exhibit 99.1</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-weight:bold;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Company Update</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-weight:bold;;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">January 18, 2019</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-style:italic;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">This morning, the following email was sent to all Tesla employees:</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">As we all experienced first-hand, last year was the most challenging in Tesla&#8217;s history.&#160;However, thanks to your efforts, 2018 was also the most successful year in Tesla&#8217;s history: we delivered almost as many cars as we did in all of 2017 in the last quarter alone and&#160;nearly as many cars last year as&#160;we did in all the prior years of Tesla&#8217;s existence combined! Model 3 also became the best-selling premium vehicle of 2018 in the US. This is truly remarkable and something that few thought possible just a short time ago.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Looking ahead at our mission of accelerating the advent of sustainable transport and energy, which is&#160;important for&#160;all life on Earth, we face an extremely difficult challenge: making our cars, batteries and solar products cost-competitive with fossil fuels. While we have made great progress, our products are still too expensive for most people. Tesla has only been producing cars for about a decade and we&#8217;re up against massive,&#160;entrenched competitors.&#160;The net effect is that Tesla must work much harder than other&#160;manufacturers to survive while building affordable, sustainable products.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">In Q3 last year, we were able to make a 4% profit.&#160;While&#160;small&#160;by most standards, I would still consider this our first meaningful&#160;profit in the 15 years since we created Tesla. However, that was in&#160;part the result of preferentially selling higher priced Model 3 variants in North America. In Q4, preliminary, unaudited results indicate that we again made a GAAP profit, but&#160;less than Q3.&#160;This quarter, as with Q3,&#160;shipment of higher priced Model 3 variants&#160;(this time&#160;to Europe and Asia) will hopefully allow us, with great difficulty,&#160;effort and some luck,&#160;to target a tiny profit.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">However, starting around May, we will&#160;need to deliver&#160;at least&#160;the mid-range&#160;Model 3 variant in all markets, as we need to reach more customers who can afford our vehicles. Moreover, we need to continue making progress towards&#160;lower priced variants of&#160;Model 3. Right now,&#160;our&#160;most affordable&#160;offering is&#160;the mid-range (264 mile)&#160;Model 3 with premium sound and interior at $44k. The need for a lower priced&#160;variants of Model 3&#160;becomes even&#160;greater&#160;on July 1, when the US tax credit again drops in half, making our car $1,875 more expensive, and again at the end of the year when it goes away entirely. </p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Sorry for all these numbers, but I want to make sure that you know all the facts and figures and&#160;understand that the road ahead is very difficult. This is not new for us &#8211; we have always faced significant challenges &#8211; but it is the reality we face. There are many companies that can offer a better work-life balance, because they are larger and more mature or in industries that are not so voraciously competitive. Attempting to build affordable&#160;clean energy products at scale necessarily requires extreme effort and relentless creativity, but succeeding in our mission is essential to ensure that the future is good, so we must do everything we can to advance the cause.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">As a result of the above, we unfortunately have no choice but&#160;to reduce full-time employee headcount by approximately 7% (we grew by 30% last year, which is more than&#160;we can support)&#160;and retain only the most critical temps and contractors.&#160;Tesla will&#160;need to make these cuts while increasing the&#160;Model 3 production rate and making many manufacturing&#160;engineering improvements&#160;in the coming months. Higher volume and&#160;manufacturing&#160;design improvements&#160;are&#160;crucial for Tesla to achieve the economies of scale required to manufacture&#160;the standard range (220 mile), standard&#160;interior Model 3 at $35k and&#160;still be&#160;a viable company. There isn't any other way.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">To those departing, thank you for everything you have done to advance our mission. I am deeply grateful for your contributions to Tesla. We would not be&#160;where we are&#160;today without you. </p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<hr style="page-break-after:always;width:100%;">
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;"><a name="_AEIOULastRenderedPageBreakAEIOU2"></a><font style="color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">For those remaining, although there are many challenges ahead, I believe we have the most exciting product roadmap of any consumer product company in the world. Full&#160;self-driving,&#160;Model Y, Semi, Truck and Roadster on the vehicle side and&#160;Powerwall/pack and&#160;Solar Roof&#160;on the energy side are only the start. </font></p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;color:#000000;font-size:11pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">&#160;</p>
